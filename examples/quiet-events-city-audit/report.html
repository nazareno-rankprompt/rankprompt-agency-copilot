The mechanism: because the city pages are thin, near-duplicate, and disco-framed, AI discounts them and falls back to Quiet Events' stronger homepage — while the competitor's longer, conference-keyword-dense, keyword-titled location pages win the citation. Miami and Orlando happen to clear the bar; Vegas and New York's identical pages clear nothing (0). It's a page-quality and intent problem, not a page-existence problem.

What to do — rebuild each city page as a local silent-events hub

The pages feel weak because they're thin, near-duplicate templates pointed at one use case. The highest-leverage fix isn't to narrow them to conferences — it's to turn each into a genuine {city} hub that covers every way Quiet Events serves that market: public events & tickets, rentals (weddings / parties / fitness), and conferences. One deep, unique page wins rental, event and conference queries at once, where a thin page wins none — and it finally gives each city page real, non-duplicate content.

Recommended city-page blueprint

Each city page should lead with the local angle, then layer the service sections. Sections double as the headings AI extracts.

The technical must-dos that make it actually get cited

  1. Make every section genuinely unique per city — real venues, real local events, real numbers. The core problem today is byte-for-byte duplication (Las Vegas = Miami verbatim). Aim past ~1,500 words of distinct content.
  2. Tune the title + meta to the hub, not "disco" alone — e.g. "Silent Disco in {city} — Events, Rentals & Conferences | Quiet Events". Kill the "No Events Yet in Your City?" meta description.
  3. Add structured data per page: LocalBusiness + Service + Event + FAQPage (today the pages carry Organization / Image / Video only).
  4. Link & surface them: internal links from the homepage and the service hubs, and add the city pages to the AI sitemap (today sitemap_agentic_discovery.xml lists only agents.md).
  5. Roll out by need: start with the two zero-cite markets — Las Vegas & New York — then the weak cluster (Washington DC, Denver, Chicago, Atlanta). Re-run this audit monthly (the 10 reports already exist) and track own-site citation share from 5.6% → 15–20%.
