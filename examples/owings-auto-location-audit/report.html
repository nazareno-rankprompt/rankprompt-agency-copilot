What we measured

We asked the same 15 buyer-intent prompts about used-car / financing options in each of Owings Auto's two markets — Arlington and Fort Worth — on 8 AI assistants, every week since April. 15 prompts × 8 platforms = 120 answers per location per week. Prompts span 3 categories: Buy Here Pay Here, Flexible Monthly Payments, and Used Car Dealership. Visibility = the share of a location's answers that mention Owings Auto.

Visibility over time — Arlington wins, Fort Worth is catching up

Arlington has held a steady 32–43 since April. Fort Worth was volatile and low (10–17) through mid-May, then jumped to ~31 — the new Fort Worth blog kicking in. The gap is closing, but Arlington still leads.

Latest visibility, by category

Owings wins on its niche — Buy Here Pay Here and flexible-payment queries — in both markets. The generic "Used Car Dealership" category is the weakest (18% in both): too broad and crowded to win on brand alone.

Why Owings wins Arlington but not Fort Worth

Unlike a thin-content problem, Owings' Fort Worth pages are actually good — its Fort Worth blog is cited 22× (more than Arlington's 14×) and it has DFW / Buy-Here-Pay-Here location pages. The gap is off-site authority and entity footprint, not on-page content.

SignalArlington (winning)Fort Worth (lagging)

Third-party profiles citing Owings9 sites, ~25 citations (Facebook, Yelp, CarEdge, BBB, Birdeye, MapQuest, Indeed, Instagram, AutosToday)2 citations (and one is the Arlington Yelp page)
Own contentstrongalso strong — blog cited 22×, DFW + BHPH pages exist
Local competitorsbeatable (5 Stars, AutoMax)entrenched (Luxury Auto 63, Russell Smith 56, Cowtown 41)

The mechanism: Arlington is Owings' home market — they're the top-named dealer, the homepage is strongly tied to the city, and the brand is corroborated across the whole review/directory ecosystem AI pulls from. Fort Worth is a contested satellite: the on-page content is fine, but with almost no off-site footprint and three entrenched incumbents, AI doesn't corroborate Owings as a Fort Worth dealer. The lever is off-site, not on-page.

Recommendations

  1. Build Fort Worth's off-site profile footprint. Get Owings listed and complete on the exact platforms that corroborate Arlington — CarEdge, BBB, Yelp, Birdeye, MapQuest, AutosToday, Cars.com / CarGurus / DealerRater — with the Fort Worth location, hours, and inventory. Today Fort Worth has essentially zero third-party citations; Arlington has nine profiles.
  2. Drive Fort Worth reviews. Review sites (Yelp, BBB, Birdeye, Google) are heavily cited. Run a review push for Fort Worth customers so the location accrues its own corroborated reputation, not Arlington's.
  3. Fix the split-listing problem. AI sometimes cites the Arlington Yelp/profile under Fort Worth queries — make sure a distinct Fort Worth (or DFW) profile exists and is claimed so the entity is unambiguous.
  4. Keep expanding the local-blog pattern — it's working. The Fort Worth "best bad-credit dealerships" blog is the single most-cited Owings page there (22×). Replicate it for long-tail Fort Worth / DFW neighborhoods and BHPH sub-topics (they already have HEB, Grand Prairie pages).
  5. Don't chase the generic "used car dealership" category. It's 18% everywhere and dominated by volume players. Double down on the Buy-Here-Pay-Here / bad-credit / flexible-payment niche where Owings already wins.
  6. Re-check monthly (the weekly reports already run). Track Fort Worth's rank (#4 → top 2) and third-party citation count (2 → match Arlington's 9) as the KPIs.
