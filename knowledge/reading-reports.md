# Reading your reports — the metrics, explained simply

`brain.md` is the reasoning. This is the gentle version: what each number means, so the diagnosis makes sense.

## One category (or location) at a time

A report covers several categories — and if you track multiple markets, several locations. Each behaves differently: a different visibility score, different sites AI cites, different page types that win. So don't read the report as one blended number; break it down per category (and per location), and pick **one** to focus on first — usually where you already have a toehold, or the one that matters most to your business. Everything below is read *within* that one segment.

## Visibility vs. citation share — the two that matter most

These sound alike and are completely different. Hold them apart.

- **Visibility (mention rate)** — across all the AI answers for your prompts, how often does AI *mention your brand at all?* "We came up in 30% of answers" is a visibility number.
- **Citation share** — of all the sources AI *cited*, how many were *your own pages?* This asks: is AI using **your** site as the source, or just name-dropping you while citing someone else?

The gap between them is usually the whole story:
- **High visibility, low citation share** = AI talks about you but trusts other people's pages for the facts. Your pages are weak, off-topic, or you lack outside corroboration. (Very common.)
- **High both** = you're winning; defend and expand.
- **Low both** = you're barely present, or you're not even tracking the questions your buyers ask.

## Rank among competitors

In each answer AI names a set of brands. Your **rank** is where you sit among them. It tells you whether you're chasing one or two leaders or fighting in a crowd — which changes how aggressive the plan needs to be.

## Platform split

The AI platforms fall into two groups:
- **Search-grounded** (Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT Search, Claude Search, Perplexity) read the live web and cite as they answer.
- **Model-native** (Grok, Gemini, base ChatGPT/Claude) lean on what they already learned.

If you're strong on model-native but weak on search-grounded, AI "knows" you but can't find pages/mentions to cite — that's a content-and-corroboration gap, not an awareness gap.

## Citation-source mix

First, look at the **most-cited sources, ranked by how often AI actually cites them** (frequency), for each category — the real top 10–20. That list *is* the answer to "where does AI get its answers here?" (One caution: rank by **frequency**, not by the *opportunity score* — the score is built to flag listicles worth pitching, so a score-sorted list is always listicles and hides what AI truly reads most. Frequency is the honest picture; the score comes later, only when choosing outreach targets.)

Then notice the *type* of each cited link — your own site, a competitor, a review site, a directory, **Reddit/forum**, social/**YouTube**, or a publisher. The **shape** of that mix is a diagnosis:
- Lots of review/directory citations → the category is won on third-party profiles.
- Lots of Reddit/forum/social/video → the category is answered from communities (a specific play — see `reddit-social.md`).
- Mostly competitor pages → they out-page you (and if their *own* product pages are cited a lot, that's proof your own pages can be cited too — they just aren't yet).
- Mostly your own pages → you're the source.

## The answer text — how the model breaks your topic apart

Each result includes the **full answer** AI gave for the prompt (plus the citations and the brands it named). RankPrompt doesn't hand you a separate list of the model's sub-searches — so read the answer itself: the sub-headings it uses, the dimensions it compares on, the brands it groups together. That's the model showing you how it breaks your topic apart. Treat it as free, high-confidence material: turn those sub-angles into the sections of your content, into new keywords, and into new prompts to track.

## What you do with all this

You read these in order, and they point at **one** root cause (`brain.md` names the patterns). You don't fix everything — you fix the one thing the numbers are pointing at, then measure whether it moved.
