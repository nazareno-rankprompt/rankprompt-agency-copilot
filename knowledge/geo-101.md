# GEO in plain English

Read this first. It's the whole game in two minutes, before any numbers.

## What changed

People used to find businesses by typing into Google and clicking links. Now a huge share of that starts with a question to an AI assistant — "what's the best CRM for a small agency?", "buy here pay here dealers near me", "best silent conference company". The AI doesn't hand back ten blue links. It **gives one answer**, naming a few brands and pulling its facts from a handful of sources.

**GEO** (Generative Engine Optimization) is the work of becoming one of those named, cited brands. If AI doesn't mention you, you're invisible at the exact moment someone is choosing.

## The one distinction to hold onto

There are two halves to this, and almost every decision comes down to which half you're working on.

### Internal GEO — what you control
Everything on your own property:
- Your **website** and its pages — do they exist, are they about the right things, can AI read them?
- The **right content** — pages that directly answer the questions buyers ask AI.
- Your **LLM file** (`llms.txt`) and an LLM-info page — a plain brief that tells AI what you are, who you're for, and why you're different.
- **Technical basics** — a clear H1, real content, schema, and pages AI crawlers are actually allowed to read.

Internal GEO is you making yourself easy to understand and quote.

### External GEO — what others say about you
AI trusts a brand more when *other* sources back it up:
- Getting **mentioned in the listicles and review sites** AI already cites ("best X for Y" articles, G2, Capterra, Yelp).
- Being **present in the communities** AI pulls from — Reddit threads, forums, sometimes LinkedIn and YouTube.

External GEO is the rest of the internet vouching for you. AI rarely recommends a brand only it has heard of from that brand's own website. It wants **consensus** — the same answer echoed across many independent places.

## It boils down to three moves

Whatever your situation, the work is some mix of these three:

1. **Write the right content** — pages that answer the exact questions buyers ask AI, in the format AI already cites for that topic. *(Internal)*
2. **Engage in social/community** — show up where AI sources its answers (Reddit and the like), genuinely and consistently. *(External)*
3. **Get mentioned externally** — earn your way into the listicles, reviews, and directories AI trusts. *(External)*

## Why a tool, not a guess

You can't tell which of these you need by intuition. You have to **look at what AI actually does** for your category — who it names, whose pages it cites, where those sources come from. That's what RankPrompt measures, and it's why this copilot starts by reading your real data before recommending a single thing.

The rest of the journey is: see where you stand → find the one real reason you're losing → make the matching move → prove it worked. One step at a time.
