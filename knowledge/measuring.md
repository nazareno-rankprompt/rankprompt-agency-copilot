# Measuring — prove it actually worked

Doing the work is half the job. The other half is proving it moved the needle. Without this, you're guessing.

## A single report is a snapshot

Run a report once and you see your visibility *right now* — one point in time. Great for diagnosis, but blind to direction: you can't tell if a number is climbing, sliding, or stuck.

## Scheduling turns the snapshot into a trend

Schedule the same report (daily / weekly / monthly) and each run adds a point to a time series. Now you can watch visibility move — and, crucially, watch it move *after* you publish content or earn a mention. A flat line says the lever didn't land; a rising line says it's working. That's how you separate "we did stuff" from "we got results."

In RankPrompt: create a scheduled report for the prompts you're working, and track it over time via its analytics and prompt-history.

## Isolate to attribute

The trick is to **isolate** what you measure. Watch the *specific* prompts or category you acted on — not a blurred average across everything. The workflow:

1. Pick the prompts you're about to work (a category, a market, a single high-value question).
2. Schedule them so they run on a cadence.
3. Publish the content, or earn the mention/Reddit thread, aimed at *those* prompts.
4. Watch that isolated set over the next runs. If it lifts, the lever worked. If it stays flat after a fair window, re-diagnose — wrong format, the page isn't crawlable, or the gap was really off-page.

Measuring one category you changed tells you something. Measuring the whole account tells you almost nothing — the signal drowns in noise.

## Citations move too

You can also isolate a *single* citation/source and watch whether AI picks it up more — or less — over time. That tells you whether a page you built or a mention you earned is actually gaining traction as a cited source, not just whether it exists. A new page that climbs from cited-once to cited-across-platforms is your work compounding; a citation that fades is a signal to refresh it or reinforce it with more corroboration (`citable-content.md` → digital consensus).

## The loop

Diagnose → ship → schedule + isolate → confirm the lift or re-diagnose. When someone asks "is this working?", the answer is never a vibe — it's the isolated, scheduled track for the prompts you changed.
